Why this matters

The situation

The United States has been escalating military posturing toward Iran. Strikes on Iranian-backed forces, carrier deployments, and authorization debates in Congress signal a trajectory that could lead to a wider war in the Middle East.

Congressional representatives have the constitutional authority — and responsibility — to weigh in. But they respond to constituent pressure. Silence reads as consent.

This site exists to make it easy for New Yorkers to be heard. We are not a political organization. We take no position on party affiliation. We take one position: diplomatic solutions must be exhausted before military force is used, and Congress must vote before any war begins.
